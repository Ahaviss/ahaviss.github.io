Cricket

Main Info

Cricket is a game played with a bat and ball. There are 2 teams each with 11 players. There are 3 types of players. An batter, an bowler and an all-rounder. The bowler bowls the ball to the batter. The batter tries to hit the ball as far as he can. An all-rounder is a batter and bowler combined. Each team's objective is to score the most runs. The team that is batting is trying to hit runs or reach the target amount of runs set by the bowling team. The bowling team is trying to restrict them from hitting a lot of runs or they are trying to stop them from hitting the target amount of runs that they set. Also, the bowling team is trying to hit the wickets which are three stumps behind the batter. Whoever scores the most runs wins!

(Fun Fact: Cricket is the second most popular sport in the world with over 2.5 Billion Fans!

Bowling

There are mainly two types of bowlers, fast and spin bowlers. Fast bowlers are bowlers which bowl with speed and pace. In fast bowlers, there are two types of bowling styles , swing and seam. Swing bowlers make the bal either swing in the sir or swing on impact. This is caused when the bowler makes one side of the ball shine and the other side rough so the air current curls around the shiny/clean side which makes the ball go the other way. Seam bowlers change the ball's angle as there is a dividing line so when that dividing line if facing the left, it goes to the left and when faced to the right, it goes to the right. Spin bowlers use something called side-spin which is when a bowlers spins the ball in one direction and the ball goes the way it was spinned due to friction.

(Another Fun Fact: Spin bowlers are the number 1 wicket takers in cricket!

Batting

While batting, you need to be precise and you need to time your shots. You also need hand-eye coordination so that you don't miss the ball and possibly give away a wicket. As a batter, you have to adapt to various playing condition and also you need to adapt to the pitch. Keep in mind that batting in Cricket is all about hand-eye coordination, timing, power, and preciseness.
